Topic: Looping (Control Flow)
Definition:
        Looping is a programming concept used to repeat a specific block of code multiple times until a certain condition is met. It prevents the need to write the same line of code over and over again.

Real-World Example:

        Think of a Fan or a Clock.

A fan doesn't just spin once and stop; it "loops" the motion of the blades continuously as long as the power is "ON" (the condition). Once you flip the switch to "OFF," the loop breaks, and the fan stops.

Use Case:
        Looping is used in almost every digital interaction:

Music Playlists:
        Using a "Repeat" function to play a list of songs from start to finish.

Login Attempts:
        A system that lets you try your password 3 times before locking you out (a loop that runs 3 times).

Data Processing: 
        Checking thousands of customer records one by one to find who has a birthday today
